What's Going Wrong
Four issues, ranked by how much they distort our marketing metrics.
⚠

We can't tell where 35% of visitors came from

Over a third of website sessions are missing the tracking tags (UTM parameters) that tell us whether a visitor came from a Google ad, a Meta ad, email, or typed in the URL directly. These all get lumped into "direct," making paid channels look worse than they are.

42
sessions affected
⚠

The signup event is broken in 2 out of 3 places

When someone signs up, the event fires from three different places in the code. One of them doesn't record who the user is, so those signups vanish from our data entirely. Another one is missing key fields. Only one of the three works correctly.

3
users permanently lost
⚠

6 signups happened in sessions we can't see

Six users went through the entire funnel and signed up, but their website sessions were never recorded. We know they converted (from the signup event), but we can't connect them to any ad campaign, landing page, or traffic source.

6
conversions unattributable
⚠

The same campaign shows up as 3 different campaigns

Our egg-freezing campaign appears as "egg_freezing_q4," "egg-freezing-q4," and "EggFreezingQ4" in the data. Dashboards split what should be one campaign into three rows, each showing a fraction of the real traffic.

3x
campaign fragmentation
The Fix
Four actions, in order. The first two are urgent. All four can be completed within 4 weeks.
1

Move signup tracking to one place in the code

Instead of three different code paths firing the signup event with different data, consolidate to a single backend endpoint that always records the user ID and plan type.

Owner: Engineering · Recovers 3 lost users
3
days
2

Capture tracking tags before they get stripped

Save UTM parameters on the server the moment someone lands on the site, before redirects, ad blockers, or cross-domain navigation can remove them.

Owner: Engineering · Cuts blind spot from 35% to under 10%
2
weeks
3

Fix the session recording gap

The server sometimes fails to log a session record even though the user's activity is tracked. Sync the two systems so every real visit has a session we can attribute.

Owner: Engineering · Recovers 6 unattributable conversions
1
week
4

Standardize campaign naming with a URL builder

Give the marketing team a simple tool that generates campaign links with consistent naming. No more manual URL tagging, no more format mismatches.

Owner: Analytics Eng. + Marketing · Eliminates campaign fragmentation

days

The Bottom Line

We spent $38,900 on paid ads over the last 6 months, but we can't confidently say which channels drove conversions. Meta shows zero signups, but that might be a tracking failure, not a performance failure. Before making any budget decisions, we need to fix the instrumentation. Four weeks of engineering work gives us the data foundation to optimize with confidence.
