Can We Trust Our Numbers?

Data quality assessment and action plan · Cofertility · April 2026
What's Going Wrong
Four issues, ranked by how much they distort our marketing metrics.
⚠

We can't tell where 35% of visitors came from

Over a third of website sessions are missing the tracking tags that tell us whether a visitor came from Google, Meta, email, or typed in the URL. These all get lumped into "direct," making paid channels look worse than they are.

42
sessions affected
⚠

The signup event is broken in 2 out of 3 places

When someone signs up, the event fires from three different places in the code. One doesn't record who the user is (those signups vanish entirely). Another is missing key fields. Only one works correctly.

3
users permanently lost
⚠

6 signups happened in sessions we can't see

Six users went through the entire funnel and signed up, but their website sessions were never recorded. We know they converted, but can't connect them to any campaign or traffic source.

6
conversions unattributable
⚠

The same campaign shows up as 3 different campaigns

Our egg-freezing campaign appears as three different names in the data. Dashboards split what should be one campaign into three rows, each showing a fraction of the real traffic.

3x
campaign fragmentation

Summary

$38,900 was spent on paid acquisition over 6 months. The current data cannot reliably attribute conversions to channels: Meta shows zero signups, which could reflect a tracking gap rather than actual performance. The fixes above require approximately 6 weeks of engineering and analytics work. Once complete, channel efficiency analysis can be re-run on reliable data.
